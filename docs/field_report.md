During my field study, I visited two local poultry farms and a feed distribution point to understand how data is currently recorded and managed. Most farmers relied heavily on manual record-keeping using notebooks, while some used basic spreadsheets on mobile devices.

I observed that feed usage, larvae growth, and production outputs were recorded inconsistently. In many cases, data was either incomplete or inaccurately logged due to time constraints and lack of structured systems. Farmers often prioritized daily operations over proper documentation, leading to gaps in historical data.

One major inefficiency identified was the absence of real-time tracking. Data was typically recorded at the end of the day, increasing the likelihood of forgotten or incorrect entries. Additionally, there was no centralized system to aggregate or analyze this data, making it difficult for farmers to track performance trends or make informed decisions.

Another challenge was poor data accessibility. Records stored in notebooks were prone to damage or loss, and retrieving past information required manual searching, which was time-consuming.

These observations highlight the need for a backend system that supports simple data entry, offline capability, and automated aggregation of records. A reliable system would reduce errors, improve efficiency, and enable better decision-making for farmers.
